Team Reflection

Our insights and learnings from the heuristic evaluation process

Process Reflection

This section contains our team's reflections on the heuristic evaluation process, including challenges faced, insights gained, and lessons learned throughout the project.

Challenges Encountered

During our heuristic evaluation, we faced several challenges that tested our understanding of HCI principles and our ability to apply them effectively:

  • Maintaining objectivity when evaluating the system
  • Balancing thoroughness with time constraints
  • Reaching consensus on severity ratings for identified issues
  • Prioritizing issues when multiple heuristics were violated
  • Developing practical improvement suggestions within technical constraints

These challenges pushed us to deepen our understanding of usability principles and develop more structured evaluation approaches.

Key Insights

Throughout this project, we gained valuable insights into the heuristic evaluation process and usability assessment:

  • The importance of multiple evaluators to identify a broader range of issues
  • How different user perspectives can reveal different types of usability problems
  • The value of structured frameworks like DECIDE in guiding the evaluation process
  • The relationship between theoretical HCI principles and practical user experience
  • How seemingly minor usability issues can significantly impact overall user experience

These insights have enhanced our understanding of user-centered design and evaluation methodologies.

Lessons Learned

This heuristic evaluation project taught us several valuable lessons that will inform our future work in HCI:

  • The importance of clearly defining evaluation goals before beginning the process
  • How to effectively document usability issues for clearer communication
  • Techniques for translating identified issues into actionable improvements
  • The value of considering both novice and expert user perspectives
  • How to balance ideal solutions with practical implementation constraints

These lessons have strengthened our skills as evaluators and will guide our approach to future usability assessments.

Individual Team Member Reflections

Each team member shares their personal reflections on the heuristic evaluation process and what they learned from this project.

Borromeo, Quisha Jame P.

"For our final HCI2 project, our five-member team conducted a heuristic evaluation of a Library Management System using Jakob Nielsen’s 10 Usability Heuristics and created a web reporting site. Each member assessed the system individually, identifying key usability issues such as unclear feedback, confusing language, and lack of user control features like “cancel” or “undo.” Based on our evaluations, we proposed improvements to enhance the system’s usability. Tasks were divided according to individual strengths, and we collaborated effectively to complete the project. Overall, it was a valuable and rewarding hands-on learning experience. "

Brito, Mark Joseph R.

"The most valuable lesson I learned from this project was the importance of structured evaluation frameworks. The DECIDE framework gave us a clear path to follow, which helped us maintain focus and thoroughness throughout the evaluation process. I also gained a deeper appreciation for the collaborative nature of usability evaluation—each team member brought unique perspectives that enriched our findings."

Primo, Sherilyn A.

"This project challenged me to think critically about what makes an interface truly usable. I found myself constantly asking 'Why would a user struggle here?' rather than just identifying that they might struggle. This shift in perspective helped me develop more thoughtful and effective improvement suggestions. I've also gained valuable skills in documenting usability issues in ways that are clear and actionable for development teams."

Pulla, Alvin

"I found the library system useful, but it still needs improvements. It was able to handle basic tasks like adding books. However, I encountered a problem: I could add a book once, but if I tried to add it again, an error would occur, preventing the action. I also noticed issues such as missing feedback messages after certain actions, confusing warnings, and unclear error messages. Some expected features were not fully functional, and the interface could be more user-friendly and consistent. As a group, we learned how to properly evaluate a system using usability principles. Each of us evaluated the system to test different features, point out issues, and suggest improvements. This helped us better understand the importance of user experience and how small changes can make a big difference. It also gave us experience in teamwork, communication, and critical thinking while reviewing a real system."

Verdejo, Marjorie P.

"The Library System was useful but still needs for improvements, provides a foundational of structure for managing books and users, its shortcomings highlight the critical of importance of user-centered of design and continuous of improvement. The system’s ability is o perform a basic tasks, starting point, but the issues encountered- missing feedback, confusing warnings, unclear errors, and inconsistent of interface. As a group member, we learned to value the systematic approach to identifying and addressing these issues. This system can experience reinforces the idea that a successful library system. It needs to be intuitive, user-friendly, and responsive needs of the users."

Future Applications

The knowledge and skills we've gained through this heuristic evaluation project will inform our future work in several ways:

  • Design Process: We'll incorporate usability heuristics earlier in the design process to prevent issues rather than just identifying them later.
  • Evaluation Methods: We'll combine heuristic evaluation with other usability testing methods for more comprehensive assessments.
  • Documentation: We'll continue to refine our approach to documenting usability issues and proposed solutions.
  • Collaboration: We'll leverage the diverse perspectives within our team for more thorough evaluations.
  • User Advocacy: We'll more confidently advocate for user needs based on established usability principles.

This project has equipped us with valuable tools and perspectives that will enhance our contributions to future user-centered design efforts.
