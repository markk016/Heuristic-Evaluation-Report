DECIDE Framework

A structured approach to guide our evaluation process

The DECIDE framework provides a structured approach to planning, conducting, and analyzing usability evaluations. Our team followed this framework to ensure a comprehensive and methodical evaluation of the system.

D

Determine the goals

In this initial step, we established clear objectives for our evaluation:

  • Identify usability issues that affect user experience
  • Evaluate the system's compliance with Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics
  • Determine the severity of identified issues
  • Establish a foundation for proposing improvements

These goals guided our evaluation process and helped us maintain focus on the most critical aspects of the system's usability.

E

Explore the questions

We formulated specific questions to address during our evaluation:

  • Does the system provide clear feedback to user actions?
  • How well does the system match real-world conventions?
  • Does the system offer user control and freedom?
  • Is the system consistent in its design and functionality?
  • How effectively does the system prevent errors?
  • Does the system minimize user memory load?
  • How flexible and efficient is the system for different user types?
  • Is the interface aesthetic and minimalist?
  • How well does the system help users recognize and recover from errors?
  • Is help documentation available and effective?

C

Choose the evaluation approach and methods

For our evaluation, we selected the following approaches:

  • Heuristic Evaluation: Using Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics as our primary framework
  • Task-Based Analysis: Evaluating the system through a set of predefined user tasks
  • Severity Rating: Assessing the impact of each identified issue on a scale of 1-4

These methods were chosen for their effectiveness in identifying usability issues without requiring end-user participation, making them suitable for our project constraints.

I

Identify the practical issues

We addressed several practical considerations for our evaluation:

  • Evaluator Selection: Team members with varying levels of expertise in HCI principles
  • Time Constraints: Allocated specific timeframes for each evaluation session
  • Equipment: Used consistent testing environments across evaluators
  • Documentation: Standardized templates for recording observations
  • Task Selection: Developed realistic tasks that cover key system functionalities

D

Decide how to deal with ethical issues

We considered the following ethical aspects in our evaluation:

  • Privacy: Ensuring that any user data encountered during evaluation remains confidential
  • Objectivity: Maintaining impartial assessment regardless of personal preferences
  • Constructive Criticism: Focusing on providing helpful feedback rather than merely criticizing
  • Intellectual Property: Respecting the system's design and implementation
  • Transparency: Clearly documenting our evaluation methods and criteria

E

Evaluate, interpret, and present the data

Our approach to analyzing and presenting the evaluation results included:

  • Data Collection: Systematically recording observations during evaluation sessions
  • Issue Categorization: Grouping findings according to Nielsen's heuristics
  • Severity Assessment: Rating each issue based on its impact on usability
  • Pattern Identification: Recognizing recurring issues across different system components
  • Visualization: Using screenshots and annotations to illustrate problems
  • Recommendation Development: Formulating evidence-based suggestions for improvement
  • Report Creation: Compiling findings into this comprehensive web report
